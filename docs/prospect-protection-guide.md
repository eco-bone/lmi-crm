# Prospect Protection — How It Works

When you add a prospect to the CRM, the system automatically **protects** that prospect for you. Protection means no other representative can pursue the same company — it is yours to work. This guide explains what protection means, how long it lasts, and what you need to do to keep it.

---

## What Does "Protected" Mean?

A protected prospect is exclusively assigned to you (and your licensee). Other representatives cannot claim or contact a protected prospect. The system tracks your engagement through **meetings** — every meeting you log is a signal that you are actively working the relationship.

There are three protection statuses:

| Status | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Protected** | Prospect is yours. Active and in good standing. |
| **Provisional** | Prospect is outside your licensee's city and pending admin approval. You can still work it, but it is not confirmed until approved. |
| **Unprotected** | Protection has lapsed. The prospect is available to anyone. |

---

## Protection by Program Type

Protection duration depends on the program type selected when the prospect is created.

### One-to-One (O2O) and Showcase (SHC) — Permanent Protection

These prospects are protected **indefinitely**. There is no expiry, no grace period, and no activity requirement after your first meeting. Once protected, they stay yours.

The only thing that can remove protection is admin action or deletion.

### Large In-House (LI) — 12-Month Active Period

Your protection is active for **12 months** from the date of your last recorded meeting.

- If you go 12 months without logging a meeting, you enter a **3-month grace period**.
- If you still log no meeting during the grace period, the prospect becomes **Unprotected** at the 15-month mark.
- Every time you log a meeting, the 12-month clock resets from that date.

### Small In-House (SI) — 6-Month Active Period

Your protection is active for **6 months** from the date of your last recorded meeting.

- If you go 6 months without logging a meeting, you enter a **3-month grace period**.
- If you still log no meeting during the grace period, the prospect becomes **Unprotected** at the 9-month mark.
- Every time you log a meeting, the 6-month clock resets from that date.

---

## The First-Meeting Rule (All Program Types)

Regardless of program type, every prospect has a **75-day first-meeting window** starting from the entry date.

| Day | What Happens |
|---|---|
| Day 1 | Prospect is created and protection begins. |
| Day 45 | If no first meeting is logged, you receive a **warning email**. |
| Day 75 | If still no first meeting, protection is **automatically removed**. |

> **Important:** Log your first meeting as soon as it happens. This is the single most important step to securing your protection.

Once your first meeting is recorded, the first-meeting window no longer applies. For LI and SI, the ongoing activity clock takes over from that point.

---

## Keeping Your Protection — Quick Reference

| Program | Log First Meeting By | Keep Meeting Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| O2O | Day 75 | Not required after first meeting |
| SHC | Day 75 | Not required after first meeting |
| LI | Day 75 | At least once every 12 months |
| SI | Day 75 | At least once every 6 months |

Every meeting you log in the system resets the activity clock. You do not need a formal sales meeting — any logged interaction counts.

---

## Grace Period

When your LI or SI prospect enters a grace period, you and your licensee will receive an **email notification**. The prospect remains protected during the grace period — you have 3 months to log a meeting and restore your full timeline.

If you log a meeting during the grace period, the prospect returns to active protection and the base-period clock starts over.

---

## Requesting a Protection Extension

If you need more time, you or your licensee can submit a **protection extension request** through the CRM. An admin will review and approve or reject the request. If approved, additional months are added to your protection period.

You cannot submit a new extension request while one is already pending.

---

## What Admins Can Do

Admins can:

- **Extend** your protection period directly or by approving an extension request.
- **Override** protection status manually (e.g. restore a lapsed prospect).
- **Reassign** a prospect to a different licensee or associate.
- **Approve or reject** provisional prospects flagged for being outside your city.

All admin actions are visible in the audit log.

---

## Notifications You Will Receive

The system sends automated emails at key moments:

| Trigger | Who Gets Notified |
|---|---|
| Day 45 — no first meeting recorded | You and your licensee |
| Day 75 — protection removed (no first meeting) | You and your licensee |
| Grace period begins (LI/SI inactivity) | You and your licensee |
| Grace period expired — prospect unprotected | You and your licensee |
| Protection extension request approved or rejected | Handled by admin |

Check your email regularly. Warnings give you time to act before protection is lost.

---

## Summary

- **Add your prospect** — protection starts immediately.
- **Log your first meeting within 75 days** — this is mandatory for all program types.
- **For LI/SI, keep logging meetings** — every meeting resets your clock.
- **Watch for warning emails** — they give you time to recover.
- **Request an extension** if you need more time — your licensee or you can do this from the CRM.
